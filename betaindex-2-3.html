Washington Road Usage Charge

Ready for the Road Ahead

GAS TAX

For over a century, Washington drivers have helped fund roads and bridges through a gas tax, whenever they fill up at the pump.

SUSTAINABLE TRANSPORTATION

As cars become more fuel efficient and less money is collected, we need a new way to fund our roads.

ROAD USAGE CHARGE (RUC)

That's where a pay-per-mile Road Usage Charge comes in.

Why RUC?

.

A RUC IS:

  • Not an additional tax – drivers will not be double-charged
  • A reliable source of funding that doesn't rely on consuming fossil fuels
  • A per-mile charge based on the number of miles driven (not gallons of gas purchased)
  • A more fair and equitable way to pay for roads – everyone pays for what they use regarless of what they drive

KEY RUC CONSIDERATIONS

As Washington prepares for a future RUC system to fund transportation, these three priorities must be addressed:

Privacy Protection

Offer drivers choice in reporting miles driven

Privacy Protection
Privacy protection starts with offering drivers a choice for how they can report their miles driven, including options that do not involve any technology, such as simply providing periodic odometer mileage readouts. Privacy protection is also achieved by establishing strong legal requirements that protect the personal information of drivers.

Equity

Ensure all drivers pay for miles driven, regardless of what kind of car they drive

Equity
RUC can address inequities in transportation funding by reducing the reliance on the gas tax, which puts the greatest burden on those who drive older, less-efficient cars and low-income households.

Harmonize Climate Goals

Reduce reliance on fossil fuels as the state transitions to electric cars

Harmonize Climate Goals
As our state transitions to electric cars by the next decade, driven by rules on vehicle emissions standards and new car sales, RUC can help decarbonize the state’s road transportation network by reducing our reliance on fossil fuel consumption to fund our roads and bridges. RUC can replace the gas tax as a better long-term, sustainable funding approach.
