The Eastern Transportation Coalition. Addressing myths about rural drivers, mileage-based user fees, and the fuel tax. December 2020

This blog entry provides an example of how to talk succinctly about the topic of geographic equity. This synopsis of research from the Coalition’s 2020 passenger vehicle pilot addresses perceptions that rural drivers would pay more under an MBUF model than they do under the current fuel tax structure. Additional detail on this issue can be found in the full report on the 2020 pilot.

RUC West. Fact sheet: Rural Drivers & Communities. July 2018

This four-page fact sheet/folio addresses common perceptions about equity related to rural drivers. It offers compelling graphics demonstrating research related to the number of miles driven by rural and urban drivers, as well as how vehicle type and fuel efficiency affect average charges per month with a gas tax vs a RUC.

The Eastern Transportation Coalition. Exploration of Mileage-Based User Fee Approaches for All Users. Chapter 9, Key Findings, 9.2 Real-World Pilots Reduce Privacy Concerns. 2022

This report offers communicators insights to the issues that matter most to users with concern to privacy and data security, and discusses the impact of using a pilot participant agreement to build confidence around these issues. Results from TETC’s 2020-21 multi-state passenger vehicle pilots include findings and recommendations from surveys and focus groups related to public perceptions of privacy issues and consistent data from pre- and post-pilot surveys show a significant decrease in concerns about privacy and security following participation in the pilot projects.

RUC West. Fact sheet: Data Privacy. 2018

This four-page fact sheet/folio gives a succinct and visual presentation of the common features of RUC programs with respect to how miles are measured and reported, and concerns about the sharing of personal information with states or third-party vendors. This piece emphasizes the choice drivers have to opt-in to different methods of reporting mileage and also explains the data types that are included (e.g., miles driven) and not included (e.g., driver information, location, and speed).

RUC West. Fact Sheet: Measuring Miles Beyond State Borders. How will a Road Usage Charge (RUC) be applied to interstate travel?. 2018

This RUC West fact sheet is an example of how to talk about RUC with regard to interstate travel, and how a cross-border system might work. The fact sheet explains how Washington recently tested interstate operability as part of its RUC pilot, how California similarly tested interstate operability with Oregon in 2015, and how toll systems are also thinking about how they might work across state lines.

North Carolina Trucking Association (provided by The Eastern Transportation Coalition). Bringing the Trucking Industry to the MBUF Table.. Tarheel Wheels, Spring 2021. pp. 8-11

This article frames MBUF in terms relevant to the trucking industry, targeted through an industry publication. It’s a good example of how to partner with stakeholders to target communications and build greater awareness in the trucking community. It tells the story of the Multi-State MBUF Truck Pilot conducted by TETC in 2018-19, early outreach to the industry through its Motor Carrier Working Group, and TETC’s 2020-21 National MBUF Truck Pilot.

The Eastern Transportation Coalition Exploration of Mileage-Based User Fee Approaches for All Users. Chapter 2, National Truck Pilot and Chapter 3, Motor Carrier Working Group. 2022

This portion of the TETC report provides communicators with a good overview of topics of importance to the trucking industry through pilot participant feedback and themes from stakeholder working sessions. TETC’s 2020-21 work represents the first multi-state truck pilot, focused on gaining a better understanding of how MBUF would specifically affect trucks.

Oregon Department of Transportation. OReGO Education Campaign Evaluation Reports. 2014-2020

This report provides communicators with a view into how a program’s communications planning has adapted and grown through the stages of RUC through public opinion research and continual evaluation. Follow-up research for the Oregon OReGo program has continued to focus on public awareness, attitudes and message testing.

Minnesota Department of Transportation. Mileage-Based User Fee Public Opinion Study (Phase III). December 2009

This research was conducted over a decade ago, but continues to show how many of the issues that were relevant over a decade ago are still present. This report is the third in a series of studies to understand public attitudes and awareness regarding mileage-based user fees (MBUF) and to learn how to communicate with the public regarding transportation funding and potential solutions. This series shows how the findings of public opinion work can be refined and in-turn, used to drive new research questions.

FHWA. Surface Transportation System Funding Alternatives Phase I Independent Evaluation: Cross-Cutting Report, Chapter 6. Public Outreach, Messaging, and Communication.. June 2021

This chapter from a multi-state cross-cutting evaluation of RUC pilots highlights the value of developing a targeted communications strategy involving messaging around key motivators, communicating to address public concerns and implementing a multi-pronged approach to outreach and communications. It is a good resource to consider framing of a communications strategy for any RUC stage and includes examples and comparisons of communications and outreach methods explored by different states and coalitions.

Legislative engagement

The Eastern Transportation Coalition. Testimony of Executive Director Dr. Trish Hendren before the United States Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works. April 2021


This synopsis and video of testimony offers an example of how to engage legislators around the results of pilot projects. The discussion includes highlights of successful mileage-based user fee (MBUF) implementation strategies related to public education, privacy concerns, the effect of MBUF on different geographies and socioeconomic groups, and for both passenger vehicles and trucks.

National Coalition of State Legislatures. State Road Usage Charge Toolkit . Apr. 1, 2022


This is a useful reference for comparison of different states’ approaches to RUC, which can be important information when engaging with policymakers and legislators. Fact sheets developed for each state include descriptions of project history, legislative activity, and details their RUC projects, This resource includes profiles for California, Eastern Transportation Coalition, Hawaii, Minnesota, Missouri, New Hampshire, Oregon, Utah and Washington.

Oregon Department of Transportation. Legislative Outreach Fact Sheet: OREGON’S ROAD USAGE CHARGE PROGRAM. February 2022


This fact sheet was used to engage Oregon legislators, and shows the importance of continued legislative education and engagement, even after an active program is established. In addition to background on legislative efforts since 2001 to establish the OReGo program, the brief document includes a description of how the RUC system works in Oregon, with a highlight on privacy concerns and the program’s practices to protect participants’ data.

Stakeholder Education & Outreach

Colorado Department of Transportation (CDOT). Fact sheet: Colorado Road Usage Charge. 2016

The document provides an example of a basic overview to support a RUC pilot, including defining RUC and the need to address the current transportation funding model, how RUC will address the problem, and demonstrates how the pilot program will work.

RUC West. RUC West Calculator Widget. 2022

This calculator widget is incorporated to several states’ websites and provides an interactive tool for users to estimate RUC fees in comparison to state and federal gas tax. RUCWest provides code for a RUC calculator that can be directly embedded to a website. RUCWest member states have embedded this calculator on their MBUF/RUC program websites, including "Colorado Department of Transportation and Hawaii Department of Transportation.

Pennsylvania Department of Transportation. Quiz: Test your Mileage-Based User Fee (MBUF) IQ. 2022

This is a unique interactive web feature that allows users to explore facts and issues through a quiz format. It is an alternative to a FAQ, which serves to raise awareness and educate users on critical issues. Each of the 12 questions is followed by a response, including citations of source information.

Pennsylvania Department of Transportation. Interactive Game: Can you close the gap? 2022

This educational exercise demonstrates the challenge that states have in meeting a transportation funding gap, and the tradeoffs present in use of different tools. Participants are asked to manage the transportation funding of a fictional jurisdiction manipulating the variables for mileage-based user fees, general taxes, corridor tolling and vehicle fees, building appreciation for the complexity of maintaining our infrastructure.

Hawaii Department of Transportation. Hawaii RUC Demonstration Usage Report Mailer. 2019

This mailer demonstrates an approach to raise general public awareness about RUC. It uses individual reporting to educate pilot participants and non-participants about RUC and how it would function for their current registered vehicle, based on reported safety inspection data tied to vehicle registration. The mailer includes a set of FAQs, and a call to action to complete an online survey on the pilot project website.

Hawaii Department of Transportation. Hawaii RUC Demonstration Project Community Meeting Posters and Presentation.

Community meetings are an effective tool when higher-touch education and engagement is needed. HI DOT developed materials to support a series meetings as part of its 36-month pilot demonstration project, including a fact sheet, posters and a meeting presentation with video that addressed background on the purpose and need for RUC, and introduced the statewide pilot.
