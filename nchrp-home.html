WHAT IS A ROAD USAGE CHARGE, OR RUC?


A road usage charge, or RUC, allows all users of the transportation system to help pay for it.
RUC is also frequently referred to as a mileage-based user fee (MBUF), vehicle miles traveled
(VMT) tax, or distance-based fee (DBF). Learn why so many states are exploring RUC as a
more sustainable replacement for the fuel tax.

ABOUT THE 2023 RUC GUIDEBOOK

The 2023 RUC Guidebook offers a snapshot of RUC resources and best practices.

Why we're providing this resource

Around the U.S., many states are exploring a road usage charge (RUC) program as they seek a sustainable alternative to the fuel tax. We created the 2023 RUC Guidebook to provide these states with practical tools for their RUC journey. This resource focuses on state RUC and does not address federal RUC, which will be explored in the proposed National RUC Pilot through the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA).

We understand that RUC is not a one-size-fits-all endeavor. To help states choose the path that works for them, our 2023 RUC Guidebook provides a detailed breakdown of the RUC framework, the building blocks of activities that support each stage of the framework, and the considerations, challenges, and strategies that states need to understand as they move RUC forward. The approach to RUC may be different based on the vehicle type, so strategies have been designed to accommodate passenger vehicles and commercial vehicles differently while relying on the same building blocks and methodologies.

Because the different types of RUC users play unique roles, we’ve also developed specific resources for lead RUC agencies, policymakers, communications professionals, and transportation professionals

Whether you’re just starting to study RUC or you’re ready to expand an existing RUC program, you’ll find resources within this guidebook to help you navigate the road ahead.

As part of our research, we’ve identiﬁed 9 considerations that can help states ﬁgure out how to move RUC forward:

  • Clean energy policies
  • Existing transportation policies and funding features
  • Public awareness and perception of transportation funding needs
  • Experience with RUC, if any
  • Constitutional and legal spending restrictions
  • Tax enactment requirements
  • Vehicle inspection requirements
  • Geographic features and population distribution
  • Agency organizational and revenue structures
  • An overview of the research that informs the guidebook

You can explore how these considerations might affect RUC in your state by taking the Lead RUC Agency Self-Assessment tool or by exploring the various stages of the RUC Framework.

In the Research & Planning stage, your communications team will focus on creating a communications plan that defines clear goals and objectives for RUC in your state and gathering input. As part of this process, think through how to engage the public, stakeholders, and the media. Tactics may include performing research about other RUC programs in the country, forming study groups, meeting with stakeholders, conducting public opinion research, and performing additional communications activities. In the Setup stage, your communications team will focus on developing a cohesive strategy to support a live RUC program. This means broader outreach to the general public, clear and consistent messaging, and strong coordination with the partner agencies that will share in implementation. You can build on any communications and research you started in the Research & Planning stage, or pivot as needed to adjust for evolving needs. You may find that you need to do more research to get the current pulse of the state as well as create additional materials to reflect the latest data. Finally, develop materials--like a website, FAQs, fact sheets, or social media posts--to help the public learn more about the new program.
