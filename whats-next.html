What's Next?

SYSTEMS ARE READY

Research and development of WA RUC began in 2012 to find a sustainable, long-term revenue source to replace the gas tax. A decade later, Washington is now ready to begin a transition away from the gas tax to a RUC system.

The Washington State Transportation Commission (WSTC) submitted its report of findings and recommendations to the state Legislature in 2020. Legislation was subsequently introduced and continues to be debated. During this time, our research continues, addressing questions from the public and policymakers, including:

  • How will RUC impact low-income and under-represented communities?
  • How will RUC perform long-term as a major revenue source for our roads and bridges, etc.?
  • How can RUC be implemented at a low cost?

THREE KEY FINDINGS HAVE EMERGED SO FAR

Privacy can be protected

RUC harmonizes transportation funding and climate policy objectives

RUC offers a balanced solution that minimizes household transportation costs, especially for low-income and rural drivers

PILOT RESEARCH COMING UP

Beginning in 2022, Washington drivers will have the opportunity to shape the future of RUC through direct participation in a series of various approaches to implementing a RUC system.

Self-reporting miles driven

Simulate a low-cost, easy-to-use, self-reporting RUC process.

Flexible payment plans

Explore how users could pay RUC in installments while maintaining low cost of operation.

Easy mileage exemptions

Offer easy-to-use, low-cost approaches to earning out-of-state travel deductions without any advanced technology.

Discounts for working class drivers

Explore approaches for making discounts easily available to those with the greatest need and at lower cost.

User-friendly invoices

Create simple, relatable, and accessible invoices that drivers can easily understand.

Understanding unregistered vehicles

Assess the prevalence of unregistered vehicles on Washington roads and the causes.

Vehicle transactions research

Explore existing vehicle-related transactions that serve as easier, less costly opportunities to report miles or pay a RUC.

Compatibility with other states

Host roundtables with other states to make sure customers can have familiar, compatible experiences with RUC wherever they travel.

ADVANCING RUC IN WA

CURRENT RESEARCH PROGRAM:

FORWARD DRIVE

Forward Drive is the current phase of federally funded RUC research that is focused on specific RUC implementation issues and considerations:

Innovation – How can technology, market innovations, and design improve Washingtonians’ experience with RUC?

Cost-effectiveness – How can we make the cost to the state of collecting a RUC as low as possible?

Equity – How can we make sure that a RUC program is fair for all Washington drivers?
